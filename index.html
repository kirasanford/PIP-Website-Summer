"Producing Inventory From the Peculiar"


Problem

60 million tons of produce in the United States is wasted annually mostly due to its appearance. Small bruises, ripening, and scars causes fruit and vegetables to end up in landfills. According to research done by University of Arizona, much food loss occurs in the transit process.

Mission

Nogales, Arizona holds the largest border port of entry for fresh produce imports from Mexico: receiving upwards of 6 billion pounds of produce. PIP's goal is to turn the peculiar, but edible items into jams, jellies marmalade, salsa, and guacamole.

Solution

We engage the community in 3 programs called "Jam Sessions", "Let's Salsa" and "Rock-n-Guac". We supply produce, jars, and equipment. Each participant will be able to take home their own jar of product and the knowledge to prepare their own version to reduce food waste.
